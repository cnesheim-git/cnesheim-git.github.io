Does location actually matter for gymnastics scoring in the SEC? Is home judging bias real?

Gymnastics fans have accused judges to have “home bias” for decades. According to the gymternet (the mass of internet gymnastics enthusiasts), when teams are at home, the judges are noticeably more lax with their judging and often award “gifts,” or scores that are higher than they should have been. For example, if a gymnast takes a medium step on their landing but otherwise has no other errors that is a -0.1 deduction. That routine will typically score around a 9.85 or a 9.9, but at home that routine might score a 9.95 which is an impossible score.

Well, let’s dig in to this issue and see if home judging bias is real and if it is, who benefits from it the most. To investigate this issue, we are going to dive into the SEC, noteably one of the more dominant conferences in NCAA women’s gymnastics. There are 9 SEC women’s gymnastics teams, 7 of which are currently ranked in the top 10.

In college gymnastics, most of the scores range from 9.7-10.0, 10.0 being a perfect score. Because of this tight scoring range, it is slightly difficult to see the differences between home and away scoring at first glance. However, the red bar (each event’s average) is noticeably higher for floor at home than it is when away. The other events don’t show too big of differences, so let’s hone in on floor.

Well, it sort of makes sense for floor to be scored higher at home than away. When teams are at home, they almost always compete floor last. In college gymnastics, it is also believed that as the competition goes on scores get looser. Meaning, higher scores are awarded easier for potentially worse routines than at the beginning of competition. So, as floor is last at home this makes sense.

Also, if home bias is a real thing, judges may be trying to overcompensate in the last event to ensure the home team wins. I am not saying this is what judges are doing, but it does make sense for a potential reason that only floor seems to have a noticeable difference when scored away vs at home.

So, essentially we have found that between the gymnasts individual scores, there is not a huge difference in home vs away scoring for every event except floor. But, are these results actually significant? Can we make actual conclusions about this data? I ran a significance test to determine if Location actually effects individual gymansts scores.
